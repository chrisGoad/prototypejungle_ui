Recombination: Swapping Parts.

Consider the two arrows in the graphics panel. Each is an atomic item in the sense that it is accessed as a whole; clicking on it selects the entire item. However, it has parts: the shaft and the head. Swap applies to parts as well. The lower arrow was arrived at by taking a standard arrow, and swapping out both head and shaft for entries in the "arrowHead" and "line" tabs in the catalog, respectively. Exercise: make the the upper arrow look like the lower one, via swaps. Such a modification might be called a recombination of the original.

Recombinations can be saved in your catalog, as we shall see shortly.
