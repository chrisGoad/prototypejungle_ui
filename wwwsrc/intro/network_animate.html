Connections

ProtoPedia has support for connecting shapes such as circles and squares with arrows or lines.

To see how to make a connection, click here: Note that when an arrow or line is connected to a shape, it automatically follows the shape when it is dragged around.

Once a connection has been made (as is the case for the wavy line), you can drag the connection around the periphery of the connected vertex, and the connection will remain stuck there in further moves of the vertex. .

Try it yourself!

To make a connection, drag one of the connectors into the graphics panel, and then select it. Little yellow boxes will appear. Connections are made by dragging the yellow boxes at the head or tail of the arrow (or heads or tails of the multi-headed connectors, or the ends of arrowless connectors). Drag the yellow box in question over the shape that you wish to connect to. Shapes that can accept a connection will highlight as you drag over them. Drop over the shape (release the mouse key), and the connection will be made.

After the connection is made, you can alter the connection point by dragging the relevant yellow box around the periphery of the connected shape. Elbow arrows are an exception (including the multi-in and multi-out versions): their ends are glued to the middle of their start and end nodes.
