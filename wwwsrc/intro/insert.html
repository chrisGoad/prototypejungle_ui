Inserting. A catalog of visual elements appears at the right of the screen. Elements are inserted by dragging them into the graphics panel. Once inserted, you can select an item by clicking on it. When you select an item, the catalog is replaced by a property-editing panel for the selected item. You can also resize the selected item, and drag it around. You can always bring back the catalog by clicking "Insert" in the top bar.

Replacing. Clicking on "replace" in the top bar puts the catalog in replace mode. In this mode, dragging the replacing element over the element to be replaced, and then dropping, effects the replacement. Different items have different roles and only items with compatible roles allow replacement. As you drag over them, replaceable items will be highlighted. If you drag a simple shape (such as a lozenge or circle) over a text box, it is the box, not the text, that will be replaced - because of the compatibility of roles. Using this method, all simple shapes can be swapped in as the "box" in a text box.

Exercise: Try inserting a few circles, resizing them, and replacing them with other shapes.

"Replace prototype" is just like "Replace", but replaces the prototype of the shape rather than only the shape itself. Prototypes will be explained in a moment.

Resizing Resize handles only appear over elements whose appearance in the graphics panel is sufficiently large to accomodate them. If you wish to resize a small element, simply zoom in until the resizer appears.
