What is a prototype, exactly?

Each circle in this example was derived from a common template called its prototype. Click on a circle. As mentioned earlier, you will see two boxes in the edit panel to the right, one for the particular circle you have clicked (the "instance"), and one for the prototype.

Circles B and C inherit their fill (interior color) from the prototype (blue), but for A, this property has been overriden (red). Click on circle A. You will note that only one of the instance properties appears with an asterisk: its fill. The asterisk means "overriden".

You can modify the color of A to yellow by changing the fill property of its instance. (How to use the color widget: select a color in the vertical bar, and a shade of that color in the square, then click choose. You can also enter a name of a color or an rgb value in the text box)

Now try editing the fill of the prototype. Note that this affects both B and C, but not A, since B and C inherit color from the prototype, but A does not.
