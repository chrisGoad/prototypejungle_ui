Up, Down, Wrap and Unwrap

A useful employment of your catalog is to create your own variants of basic elements, such as arrows. In the graphics panel, you see a standard arrow, and, below it, a variant. The variant was arrived at by taking a standard arrow, and swapping out both head and shaft for entries in the "arrowHead" and "connector" tabs in the catalog, respectively.

Try creating some variants of items from the box tab of the catalog.

Important notes: (1) When constructing items for use in the catalog, new variants, you should always operate on prototypes rather than instances.
