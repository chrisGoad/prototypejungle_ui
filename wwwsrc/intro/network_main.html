A network diagram is a free-form collection of nodes connected by arrows which you populate "by hand".

Inserting. A catalog of visual elements appears at the right of the screen. Elements are inserted by dragging them into the graphics panel. Once inserted, you can select an item by clicking on it. When you select an item, the catalog is replaced by a property-editing panel for the selected item and its prototype. You can also resize the selected item, and drag it around. You can always bring back the catalog by clicking "Insert" in the top bar.

Connecting.First, insert a few circles (or other shapes). New connections can be added in the following way: select any of the shapes. Note that "connect" appears as an option in the action menu on the left side of the screen. Click on connect, then select another shape. An arrow connecting the shapes will appear. The kind of connector can be changed with "replace" or "replace prototype". Try dragging the shapes around, or resizing them. Note that the arrows follow along.
